Bang Bang Chicken

    Home

Ingridients

  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless Chicken breast, cut into 1in cubes
  • 1/4 cup potato starch
  • 1/4 cup all purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon grounded black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon shichimi togarashi (Japanese red pepper condiment)
  • 2 cups oil for frying, or as needed
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoon sweet chili sauce
  • 1 teaspoon gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)

Directions

  1. Whisk together milk and egg in large bowl until well combined. Add chicken; stir and coat and allow to marinate for 15 minutes. Drain and discard egg mixture
  2. Combine potato starch, flour, salt, pepper, and shichimi in a medium bowl. pour into a large plastic resealable bag. Add chicken; seal the bag and shake until chicken is well coated
  3. Heat 2 inches oil in deep saucepan over medium heat to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C). Working in the batches of about 5 to 8 piecs at a time, cook chicken in hot oil for 90 seconds. Remove to a paper towel-line plate; keep each batch seperate
  4. Increase oil tempature to 350 degrees (175 degreees C). The oil should be continuously boiling at this point. Carefully re-add the first batch of chicken and fry for anothe 90 seconds.
  5. Remove to another paper towel-lined plate. Repeat with the remaining batches of chicken.
  6. mix together mayonnaise, honey, sweet chili sauce, and gochujang in a large serving bowl until combined.
  7. Toss fried chicken with sacue to coat, Serve and enjoy!!

Recipes Tips

You can subtitue the potato starch for cornstarch if desired.
